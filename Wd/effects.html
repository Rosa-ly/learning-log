Environmental Changes in Beijing

Since I was a child, many environmental changes have impacted our life. These changes include:

  • Air pollution and smog
  • Rising temperatures

1. Air Pollution and Smog

Beijing has long been known for its air pollution, but over the years, it has made remarkable strides in recent years to improve its air quality. However, now Beijing's air and water quality have improved greatly, soil conditions have maintained a good state and the acoustic environment remains stable.

I think the most serious time was 2013. In that year, Beijing experienced the infamous “airpocalypse,” when smog got so bad that levels of PM2.5, a microscopic pollutant, reached 900 micrograms per square meter, 90 times higher than the World Health Organization’s recommended daily level. Particulate matter (i.e., PM1.0 and PM2.5), considered as the key atmospheric pollutants, exerts negative effects on visibility, global climate, and human health by associated chemical compositions. I think the main reason is Beijing’s GDP, population, and vehicles sharply increased by 1078%, 74%, and 335% respectively.

Actually I look forward to having a holiday in primary school because of the smog (✿◡‿◡).

These smog-filled days have made outdoor activities difficult and dangerous, especially for children and the elderly. So, the government has launched some policies: traffic restrictions based on the last digit of license plate numbers. Or traffic restrictions based on even- and odd-numbered license plates. Sometimes the school was closed and stopped work because of too much smog. School outdoor activities will be cancelled during moderate pollution.

2. Rising Temperatures

Beijing's summers have become noticeably hotter since I was a child. Now this situation is getting worse and worse.

I think there are the following points.

  • The Beijing region has been experiencing the influence of a warm air mass that is relatively strong in intensity and has a prolonged impact.

  • Due to the high-pressure ridge's control, the skies are clear with only a few clouds, allowing for stronger radiative heating and further amplifying the rise in temperature.

  • The duration of daytime is at its longest around the Summer Solstice, which contributes to the occurrence and sustenance of high temperatures.

  • The low air humidity and dry weather conditions also contribute to the temperature rise.

Although we went through a difficult period. Every cloud has a silver lining. The capital's Ecology and Environment Bureau published the 2023 Beijing ecological environment report, showing that the city's natural environment has been improving steadily.
