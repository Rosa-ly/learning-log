Practical Actions to Combat Climate Change

Combatting climate change requires everyone's efforts.

  • Transform our transport

Transport accounts for around a quarter of all greenhouse gas emissions across the world. We can choose to walk or cycle whenever possible. If the road is long, choose public transport, preferably electric options.

  • Save food

    • People globally waste 1 billion tonnes of food each year, which accounts for around 8-10% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

    Store food correctly. Make compost out of inedible remnants and use it to fertilize your garden. Composting is one of the best options for managing organic waste while also reducing environmental impacts. 

  • Conserve Water

    • Conservation of water mainly refers to protect, preserve, and control the usage of water and its resources. It is the system introduced to manage freshwater, reduce the wastage and protect the water and its resources in order to reduce and to avoid the scarcity.

    We can use water-efficient appliances, turn off the tap in time when don't use water and rainwater harvesting for gardening or washing purposes.

  • Support Renewable Energy

Choose energy sources like solar or wind power and install solar panels. Choose a zero-carbon or renewable energy provider. Switch off appliances and lights when you are not using them and better yet buy the most efficient products in the first place.

  • Shop local and buy sustainable (seansonal foods)

Reducing food’s carbon footprint. It can help small local businesses and farms and reduce fossil fuel emissions associated with transport and cold chain storage. Sustainable agriculture uses up to 56% less energy, creates 64% fewer emissions and allows for greater levels of biodiversity than conventional farming.

Try growing your own fruit, vegetables and herbs.

  • Plant trees

Every year approximately 12 million hectares of forest are destroyed and this deforestation, together with agriculture and other land use changes, is responsible for roughly 25% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Trees absorb carbon dioxide and provide oxygen, which can effectively prevent global warming.
