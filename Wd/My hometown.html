 My hometown

I come from Beijing, which is the world's most populous national capital city as well as China's second largest city with more than 22 million residents. It's known for its rich culture and vibrant modern life. It is one of the most attractive cities, both ancient and modern. It has over 3,000 years of history, from the imperial palaces of the Ming and Qing dynasties to the skyscrapers and crowded streets of today, Beijing has witnessed dramatic changes over the years.

Despite its bustling urban environment, there are also many serene parks and historical sites. My hometown has many ancient buildings, such as the Temple of Heaven Park, the Great Wall, the Summer Palace, the National Stadium (the Birds Nest), the National Aquatics Centre (the Water Cube) and the Forbidden City. As the capital of China, Beijing is not only the political center but also a cultural and technological hub, constantly evolving with cutting-edge innovations while preserving its deep-rooted traditions.

Beijing's climate has four distinct seasons. Unfortunately, climate change has left its mark on my hometown. On this website, I will introduce the impact of these environmental changes on my childhood. Beijing is not just a symbol of China's rich past but also a city facing the future’s environmental challenges.
